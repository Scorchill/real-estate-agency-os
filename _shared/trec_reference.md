# TREC reference

> **Inline flag for stranger readers:** Texas-specific contract anchors as of mid-2026. TREC (the Texas Real Estate Commission) is the state agency that regulates real estate practice and publishes the standard promulgated contract forms. TREC forms and timing conventions change; verify against the current TREC promulgated forms (trec.texas.gov) before relying on any specific phrasing for a deal.

## How to use this reference

These are **interpretation patterns** for reading a TREC contract, NOT derived deadlines to apply by default. Every deadline on a deal comes from the executed contract — not from this file. The role of this reference is to teach 04 (and any specialist consulting it) **what to look for in the contract** and **what TREC's standard paragraph numbers cover**, so the agent can pull the correct values from the contract itself.

Common failure mode this prevents: a specialist treats "3 days after effective" as canonical when the contract negotiated something else, and a real deadline gets missed. **Anchor on the contract; this file teaches you to read it.**

## The effective date anchor

Most TREC deadlines are computed off the **effective date**, defined as the date the last party signs and the document is delivered. 04 should always confirm the effective date with the agent (and against the executed contract) before computing any deadline.

## Deadlines — what to look for in the contract

| Event | What to look for in the contract | Where it lives |
|---|---|---|
| Option fee delivery | Negotiated value in TREC 20-17 paragraph 5 — verify against the executed contract (commonly within a few days of effective, but read the contract) | TREC 20-17 paragraph 5 |
| End of option period | Negotiated number of days in TREC 20-17 paragraph 5; compute end date off the effective date in the executed contract (typical range 5–10 days, but the contract governs) | TREC 20-17 paragraph 5 |
| Earnest money delivery | Negotiated value in TREC 20-17 paragraph 5 — verify against the executed contract (commonly a few days after effective unless modified) | TREC 20-17 paragraph 5 |
| Seller's disclosure (residential resale) | Confirm delivery date against the executed contract and listing agreement; flag if missing | TREC OP-H |
| Title commitment receipt | Window stated in TREC 20-17 paragraph 6 — verify against the executed contract and confirm with the title company (commonly around 20 days, but read the contract) | TREC 20-17 paragraph 6 |
| Survey delivery | Negotiated terms in TREC 20-17 paragraph 6 — verify who funds and the deadline against the executed contract | TREC 20-17 paragraph 6 |
| Financing contingency | Negotiated terms in the financing addendum (TREC TAR-1901 if used) — verify against the executed contract (commonly 21–30 days, but read the contract) | TREC TAR-1901 if used |
| Closing date | Specific date stated in TREC 20-17 paragraph 9 of the executed contract | TREC 20-17 paragraph 9 |

## IABS — the legal disclosure 03 may need to reference

The **Information About Brokerage Services** form (IABS) must be presented at first substantive communication with a prospective party. 03 should not draft client emails that initiate a representation discussion without confirming IABS has been delivered (or include IABS as an attachment in the draft).

## Seller's disclosure timing

For residential resale, the seller's disclosure (TREC OP-H or equivalent) must be delivered before the contract is signed (or within a few days after, depending on the listing agreement). 04 should flag if a deal goes under contract without the disclosure on file — that's a risk.

## Option period — what 04 watches

Option period is the buyer's unrestricted right to terminate for any reason. 04 tracks:
- Option fee paid? (check)
- Option period end date (read from the executed contract — paragraph 5 specifies the negotiated number of days off the effective date)
- Inspections scheduled to complete within option period
- Any negotiation following inspection happens within the option window

After option ends, the buyer is generally locked in (subject to financing and other contingencies).

## Phrasing 04 can use when explaining to a client

> *"Your contract sets the option period through [DATE per contract]. That's your unrestricted right to back out — for any reason or no reason. Once it ends, you're locked in unless [financing/appraisal/title] gives you a way out."*

> *"Per the contract, earnest money is due to [TITLE CO] by [DATE per contract]. If it doesn't land, the seller can technically terminate."*

> *"The contract gives us [N per contract] to get the title commitment back. If anything in it surprises us, we have a window to object."*
