Either way, the result goes in a file named `voice_<your-firstname>.md` in this same folder. Speech patterns are what 03 will mimic; do NOT include client names, deal details, or PII (this file loads into every conversation). A voice profile captures *how* you say things — pace, register, phrasing — not *what you decide*. Keep strategic positions (e.g., "always ask for a credit over a repair") out of it; those are per-deal calls the agent makes at drafting time, not voice.

## Don't want to fill this in by hand?

Paste the prompt below into any chat in the project. It will interview you and produce a finished profile in this exact format — you just save the result.

```
I'm setting up my voice profile for the team's system. Interview me to build it.

Ask me a few questions at a time, conversationally — don't dump the whole list at once:
- Do my messages tend to be short and frequent, or longer and less often?
- How would I describe my tone — formal, friendly, dry, warm, direct? (combinations are fine)
- What words and phrases do I actually use a lot?
- What words, phrases, or punctuation would never sound like me?
- Walk me through how I'd write three messages: a first greeting to a new client,
  a routine follow-up, and a piece of bad news. I can give you rough versions and
  you tighten them, or describe the situation and you draft in my style as we go.
  (For the bad-news one, capture my *tone* for hard news — not how I'd handle the
  situation strategically; that's a per-deal call the agent makes, not voice.)

When you have enough, produce a complete voice profile in the exact format of
`_shared/voice/voice_TEMPLATE.md` — the same six sections (Pace, Register, Phrases I
use, Phrases I avoid, and the three Sample sections), my answers filled in, no
placeholders left. Then write the finished profile to
`_shared/voice/voice_<my-firstname>.md` in this folder — or, if I'd rather place it
myself, give it to me in a code block.

Keep it to speech patterns only — no real client names, deal details, or PII,
and no strategic positions (voice is *how* you say things, not *what you decide*).
```
