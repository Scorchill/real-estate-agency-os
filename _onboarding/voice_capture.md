# Step 2: Capture your voice

> **This is the second of three onboarding steps. Do this before the practice run** — your practice case uses your voice to draft a client email, and without your voice file, you'll see a generic fallback instead of the real personalized output.

## What this produces

A file at `_shared/voice/voice_<your-firstname>.md` capturing how *you* write to clients: pace, register, phrases you use, phrases you avoid, three sample drafts (first greeting, routine follow-up, bad news). The specialist that drafts client comms (03) reads this file every time it writes in your voice.

## What this is NOT

Your strategic positions on deals. Voice is *how* you say things, not *what you decide*. Strategy is a per-deal call you make at drafting time; it doesn't belong here. (Sample #3 captures your *tone* for bad news, not your *playbook* for handling it.)

## Two ways to do it

### Option A — Paste the interview prompt (recommended)

Open `_shared/voice/voice_TEMPLATE.md` and copy the interview prompt at the bottom (between the triple backticks). Paste it into a chat in this project. Answer the questions conversationally — Claude will draft your profile, you review, Claude writes it to `_shared/voice/voice_<your-firstname>.md`.

Time: ~15 minutes.

### Option B — Fill in the template by hand

Copy `_shared/voice/voice_TEMPLATE.md` to `_shared/voice/voice_<your-firstname>.md`. Fill in each section. Use Diana's profile (`_shared/voice/voice_diana.md`) as a worked example.

Time: ~20 minutes.
