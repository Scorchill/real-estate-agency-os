# TASK_MAP

How the four specialist folders were chosen, and which real-estate tasks live where.

## What this is

The brief specified four specialists and gave a sentence or two on what each owns. This map answers the deeper question: which underlying real-estate tasks each absorbs — and which the system deliberately leaves with the humans.

Tasks come from [O*NET 41-9022 — Real Estate Sales Agents](https://www.onetonline.org/link/summary/41-9022.00). Three groups:

- **Group 1 — in scope, named by the brief** — tasks falling under the brief's specialist descriptions
- **Group 2 — in scope, required by practice** — tasks the brief doesn't name but no working RE system can ignore
- **Group 3 — out of scope or human-only** — tasks the system should not absorb (physical, relationship-bound, or outside business model)

## Coverage at a glance

Of 33 O*NET tasks for real estate sales agents, the system handles **15 in scope** and leaves **18 out** — roughly half.

The 18 out-of-scope tasks fall into three buckets — Physical (showings, walkthroughs, attending closings), Relationship (negotiation, intermediation, networking), or Business-model mismatch (commercial, new construction, appraisal).

**Group 3 — out of scope or human-only** (18 tasks):

- **Physical (P)** — tasks 2, 6, 15, 17, 18, 22, 23: showings, walkthroughs, presenting offers in person, pre-visit inspections
- **Relationship (R)** — tasks 3, 11, 20, 25, 30: negotiation, intermediation, networking, mortgage counseling, convening meetings
- **Business-model mismatch (B)** — tasks 21, 26, 29, 31, 32, 33: new construction, appraisal, training, rentals, lender work, undeveloped land

## Why this matters for a stranger reading the repo

"Why these four folders and not different ones?" → the brief specified them.

"Could this system handle a different team's work?" → run the same exercise against the relevant O*NET occupation, sort tasks into the three groups, design folders around the in-scope ones. Diana is the first application.
